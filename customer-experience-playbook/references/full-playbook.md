# HeySalad Customer Experience Playbook

Version 1.0 · 2025 · Confidential

## Scope

This playbook defines HeySalad's operating standard for customer experience across:
- customer service
- support systems
- feedback loops
- customer journey mapping
- retention and loyalty
- NPS tracking
- complaint resolution
- customer success management
- CX reporting and tooling
- AI-powered support operations

## 1. Introduction and Philosophy

Customer Experience is the sum of every interaction, emotion, and perception a customer has
with the brand across every channel and touchpoint. It is not owned by one team. It is a
company-wide discipline and a competitive moat.

### HeySalad CX North Star

- Every customer interaction is an opportunity to build trust, demonstrate competence, and earn loyalty.
- Every complaint is a gift.
- Every piece of feedback is data for improvement.
- Experiences should feel personal, fast, and fair at any scale.
- Measure what matters, act on what is learned, and close the loop every time.

### Why CX Matters

Customer experience has overtaken price and product as a key differentiator. For HeySalad,
operating across the UK, Estonia, and Zambia in fintech and restaurant technology, trust is
not optional. Customer funds, payment flows, and merchant livelihoods depend on operationally
sound experiences.

## 2. CX Strategy and Customer-Centric Culture

### Foundational Beliefs

- Customers define quality.
- Consistency builds trust.
- Empathy is a trainable skill.
- Speed is a feature.
- Data enables improvement.
- Every team owns CX.

### CX Maturity Model

| Level | Stage | Characteristics | Key Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reactive | Fire-fighting, inconsistent process, high complaint volume | Document response procedures |
| 2 | Defined | Processes documented, SLAs set, inconsistent KPI tracking | Implement CSAT and NPS, train support staff |
| 3 | Managed | Data-driven decisions, active feedback loops, CX team exists | Automate tier-1 support, build journey maps |
| 4 | Optimised | Proactive outreach, predictive churn, high NPS | Launch loyalty and Voice of Customer |
| 5 | World-Class | CX is a moat, customers become advocates | Embed CX KPIs in executive scorecards |

### Governance

- Appoint a CX lead or assign the function to Head of Operations.
- Run a monthly CX review with product, support, engineering, and finance.
- Maintain a CX improvement backlog ranked by customer impact and effort.
- Report NPS, CSAT, CES, and FCR quarterly to leadership.
- Conduct bi-annual journey audits.

## 3. Customer Service and Support Systems

### Channel Architecture

| Channel | Audience | Use Case | Target Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| In-app chat | Consumers and merchants | Real-time support, account queries | `< 3 min` AI, `< 15 min` human |
| Email | All segments | Complex issues, documents, complaints | `< 4 hours` business hours |
| WhatsApp | Zambia and emerging markets | Payments support, mobile money queries | `< 30 minutes` |
| Phone | B2B, VIPs, escalations | Urgent, high-value support | Immediate |
| Help Centre | All | Self-serve FAQs and guides | Always available |
| Social DM | Consumer brand queries | Public complaints and PR-sensitive cases | `< 1 hour` acknowledgement |

### Tiered Support Model

- Tier 0: self-service via AI chatbot, help centre, tooltips, status notifications
- Tier 1: frontline agents for standard queries and basic complaints
- Tier 2: senior specialists for escalations, technical issues, disputes, compliance matters
- Tier 3: CX lead and leadership for executive, legal, regulatory, and media-sensitive issues

### Technology Stack

- Ticketing: Zendesk, Freshdesk, or Intercom
- CRM: linked customer profile and support history
- AI: Sally at the app layer
- Status page for outage deflection
- Searchable, multilingual knowledge base
- Analytics for volume, resolution time, FCR, and CSAT

### Non-Negotiable Agent Standards

1. Greet every customer by name.
2. Avoid jargon.
3. Take ownership.
4. Set clear expectations.
5. Confirm resolution before closing.

## 4. Customer Journey Mapping

### Six Journey Stages

| Stage | Customer Goal | Key Touchpoints | Main CX Risk / Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Discover a solution | Social, search, app stores, word of mouth | First impression and clarity |
| Consideration | Assess fit and trust | Website, reviews, demos, sales calls | Trust signals and simplicity |
| Onboarding | Get set up fast | App install, KYC, first transaction | Friction-driven drop-off |
| Activation | Reach first value | First payment, first order, first card use | Aha moment must land |
| Growth | Expand usage | Repeat transactions, upgrades, features | Habit formation and loyalty |
| Advocacy | Refer others | Reviews, referrals, NPS, community | Promoter conversion |

### Mapping Process

1. Define segment and scope.
2. Gather qualitative research.
3. List all touchpoints.
4. Capture action, emotion, and channel.
5. Identify pain and delight points.
6. Prioritise by frequency and impact.
7. Update quarterly or after major changes.

### Omnichannel Continuity

- Unified customer record
- Consistent tone and standards
- Warm handovers
- Chatbot session context passed to humans

## 5. Feedback Loops and Voice of Customer

### Closed-Loop Architecture

| Stage | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Collect | Gather CSAT, NPS, CES, app feedback, reviews, social mentions | CX Lead |
| Analyse | Categorise by segment, sentiment, theme | CX Lead + Data |
| Route | Share with owning team | CX Lead |
| Act | Commit fixes to backlog and roadmap | Team Lead |
| Close the loop | Tell customers what changed | CX Lead + Marketing |
| Review | Run monthly and quarterly reviews | CX Lead + Leadership |

### Feedback Instruments

- CSAT after interaction or transaction
- CES after onboarding and complex resolution
- in-app micro-surveys after key actions
- review mining across app stores and public platforms

### Close-the-Loop Standard

- Detractors get personal follow-up within 48 hours
- Promoters get thanks and referral/review opportunities
- Product improvements should be communicated as customer-driven changes

## 6. NPS Tracking

### NPS Basics

NPS asks: "How likely are you to recommend HeySalad to a friend or colleague?"

- Promoters: 9-10
- Passives: 7-8
- Detractors: 0-6

NPS = `% Promoters - % Detractors`

### Programme Design

- Relational NPS quarterly across active customers
- Transactional NPS after high-stakes moments
- Keep surveys to rating + one open-ended reason
- Avoid surveying the same person more than once every 90 days
- Target at least 20% response rate

### Acting on NPS

| Segment | Profile | Immediate Action | Long-Term Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Promoters | Loyal advocates | Thank, ask for review/referral | Advisory panels, case studies |
| Passives | Satisfied but vulnerable | Ask what would make them a 10 | Education, loyalty offers |
| Detractors | At-risk, unhappy | Personal outreach within 48 hours | Root-cause analysis and monitoring |

## 7. Complaint Resolution

### Complaint Classification

| Severity | Definition | Examples | Response SLA | Resolution SLA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Legal, regulatory, financial-loss, safety | Fraud, data breach, unauthorized transaction | `< 1 hour` | `< 24 hours` |
| P2 | Major service failure | Failed payment, funds held, account locked | `< 2 hours` | `< 48 hours` |
| P3 | Recurring or meaningful friction | App bug, incorrect charge, failed refund | `< 4 hours` | `< 5 business days` |
| P4 | Minor inconvenience | UI confusion, missing feature | `< 8 hours` | `< 10 business days` |

### HEARD Framework

- Hear
- Empathise
- Apologise
- Resolve
- Diagnose

### Regulatory Complaints

- Acknowledge formal complaints in writing within the required timeframe
- Provide a final response within the jurisdictional deadline
- Maintain complaint registers
- Report complaint data to regulators where required

### Service Recovery Principle

When failure is handled exceptionally well, loyalty can exceed that of customers who never
experienced the issue. Every serious complaint should include an appropriate recovery action
and a satisfaction confirmation before closure.

## 8. Retention and Loyalty

### Retention Economics

Retention is typically far cheaper than acquisition, and even small improvements can produce
large profitability gains.

### Churn Prevention Signals

- transaction frequency drops
- unresolved billing or payment issues
- low NPS with no follow-up
- incomplete onboarding
- inactivity periods
- negative public reviews

### Intervention Playbook

- Day 1: automated help offer
- Day 3: personalized email
- Day 7: phone call for high-value accounts
- Day 14: final outreach and reason capture
- Day 30+: dormant-customer re-engagement flow

### Loyalty Programme Structure

| Tier | Criteria | Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | Account plus first transaction | Welcome reward, core feature access |
| Active | Repeat transactions or referral | Reduced fees, priority chat, early feature access |
| Power | High volume or multiple referrals | Account manager, fee caps, cashback, beta access |
| Elite | Top 5% or strategic account | White-glove support, custom SLAs, co-marketing |

### Referral Programme

- Reward both referrer and referee
- Make sharing frictionless
- Track attribution accurately
- Celebrate top referrers

## 11. AI-Powered CX

### Key Use Cases

- tier-0 automation
- smart routing
- agent assist
- sentiment analysis
- feedback tagging
- predictive churn
- personalized outreach

### Guardrails

1. AI must not handle regulated complaints alone.
2. AI must always offer human escalation.
3. AI must not impersonate a human when asked directly.
4. AI output must be reviewed for quality and bias.
5. No irreversible financial actions without human confirmation.
6. Keep humans in the loop for high-risk, distressed, or legally sensitive interactions.

## 12. Ten Commitments

1. Every customer is known by name and history.
2. Contacts are handled within published SLAs.
3. Complaints are treated with empathy and urgency.
4. Feedback loops are closed.
5. Detractors are acted on within 48 hours.
6. Complaints are resolved at the lowest competent tier.
7. Experiences are designed for the least sophisticated user in each market.
8. AI augments people, not replaces them.
9. Journey maps are reviewed at least twice per year.
10. CX metrics are reviewed by leadership and tied to company OKRs.
