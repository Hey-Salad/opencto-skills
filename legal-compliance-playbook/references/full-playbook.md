# HeySalad Legal & Compliance Playbook

Version 1.0 · 2025 · Confidential

## Scope

This playbook defines the legal and compliance operating standard for HeySalad across:
- legal and regulatory compliance
- governance and internal controls
- consumer protection and complaint handling
- AML/CFT, KYC, and sanctions controls
- privacy and data protection
- licensing and regulatory obligations
- contract and vendor risk management
- records, evidence, and audit readiness

## 1. Operating Philosophy

Legal and compliance are not paperwork functions. They are control systems that protect
customers, preserve licenses, manage risk, and create trust. The standard is operational
proof: defined obligations, named owners, working controls, audit trails, and timely
escalation.

### Core Principles

- protect customers first
- document obligations clearly
- translate obligations into controls
- maintain evidence of execution
- escalate early on high-impact risk
- distinguish legal advice from operational implementation
- keep jurisdiction-specific requirements explicit

## 2. Governance Framework

### Governance Components

- compliance owner or lead
- legal owner or external counsel relationship
- policy inventory
- risk register
- issue register
- complaints register
- incident log
- board or leadership reporting cadence
- annual review programme

### Governance Standards

- assign one accountable owner per obligation area
- define escalation thresholds
- review policy set at least annually
- track remediation actions to closure
- ensure leadership visibility into high-risk items

## 3. Regulatory Operating Model

For each regulated activity, define:

1. jurisdiction
2. regulated activity type
3. governing rule or framework
4. operational obligations
5. reporting cadence
6. evidence and retention requirement
7. escalation path for breach or delay

### Common Areas

- payments and regulated financial services
- consumer protection
- data protection
- anti-money laundering and sanctions
- complaints handling
- outsourcing and third-party oversight
- audit and assurance

## 4. Policies, Processes, Controls, Evidence

Keep these distinct:

| Layer | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Policy | States the rule or intent |
| Process | Describes how work is done |
| Control | Prevents, detects, or corrects risk |
| Evidence | Proves the process/control occurred |

### Control Design

Each control should define:
- risk addressed
- control owner
- frequency
- trigger or threshold
- record generated
- escalation on failure

### Control Types

- preventive
- detective
- corrective

## 5. Consumer Protection and Complaints

### Complaint Handling Standards

- acknowledge complaints within required timelines
- classify by severity and regulatory impact
- maintain formal complaint register
- define final response deadlines
- preserve all complaint evidence
- provide external escalation rights where required

### Complaint Severity Model

| Level | Example |
|---|---|
| Critical | fraud, unauthorized transactions, data breach, regulator complaint |
| High | funds held, payment failure causing material harm, account lockout |
| Medium | incorrect fees, unresolved billing issues, recurring friction |
| Low | dissatisfaction, UX confusion, feature complaints |

### Complaints Register Fields

- complaint ID
- date received
- complainant type
- jurisdiction
- issue category
- severity
- owner
- response deadline
- final outcome
- root cause
- remediation required

## 6. AML/CFT, KYC, and Sanctions

### Core Components

- customer due diligence
- enhanced due diligence
- beneficial ownership checks
- sanctions and PEP screening
- transaction monitoring
- suspicious activity escalation
- training and awareness
- evidence retention

### Design Questions

- who is screened and when?
- what data is required?
- what thresholds trigger review?
- who clears alerts?
- what is the escalation path?
- how long are records retained?

### Common Failures

- policies not matched by system capability
- poor alert triage ownership
- weak evidence retention
- inconsistent screening coverage
- undocumented exceptions

## 7. Data Protection and Privacy

### Required Considerations

- lawful basis
- notice and transparency
- purpose limitation
- data minimization
- retention schedule
- access control
- deletion and correction process
- third-party processor obligations
- cross-border transfer rules
- breach detection and notification

### Data Protection Operating Rules

- collect only what is necessary
- define retention before launch
- document processor relationships
- log access to sensitive data
- route privacy requests to accountable owners
- keep breach playbooks ready before incidents occur

## 8. Contracting and Legal Documentation

### Contract Review Focus

- parties and scope
- obligations and service levels
- payment and fee terms
- liability and indemnities
- confidentiality and data handling
- termination and survival
- dispute resolution
- regulatory or compliance obligations

### Contract Operations Standards

- version control
- named owner
- approval workflow
- renewal / termination dates tracked
- obligation register for signed deals

## 9. Third-Party and Vendor Risk

### Due Diligence Areas

- regulatory status
- financial stability
- security posture
- data handling
- subcontractor use
- geographic risk
- business continuity capability
- contractual protections

### Ongoing Oversight

- periodic review cadence
- incident notification obligations
- performance and SLA tracking
- control assurance where needed
- exit planning for critical vendors

## 10. Incident and Breach Management

### Incident Framework

1. identify
2. classify
3. contain
4. assess legal/regulatory impact
5. notify internal stakeholders
6. notify regulator / customer if required
7. remediate
8. document and review

### Incident Log Fields

- incident ID
- date/time
- category
- severity
- affected data/customers/systems
- owner
- containment status
- notification obligations
- closure date
- remediation actions

## 11. Audit Readiness

Audit readiness means evidence is already available before the request arrives.

### Maintain

- current policy inventory
- signed approvals
- control evidence
- register accuracy
- issue and remediation records
- training logs
- monitoring reports
- complaint and incident records
- vendor due diligence files

### Audit Questions to Prepare For

- what is the rule or obligation?
- what control addresses it?
- who owns it?
- how often is it performed?
- what evidence proves it?
- how are failures escalated and fixed?

## 12. Reporting to Leadership

Report on a defined cadence:

| Cadence | Content |
|---|---|
| Monthly | complaints, incidents, open issues, remediation status |
| Quarterly | compliance status, policy reviews, top risks, audit readiness |
| Annual | full framework review, major changes, training completion, assurance summary |

### Useful Metrics

- complaint volume and aging
- regulatory response timeliness
- issue remediation aging
- control completion rates
- training completion
- screening alert closure times
- vendor review completion
- privacy request turnaround

## 14. Non-Negotiables

1. No regulated obligation without a named owner.
2. No control without evidence.
3. No incident without classification and log entry.
4. No complaint without deadline tracking.
5. No policy without review cadence.
6. No vendor onboarding without due diligence proportionate to risk.
7. No privacy or financial-crime process without escalation rules.
8. No executive reporting without open-issue visibility.
