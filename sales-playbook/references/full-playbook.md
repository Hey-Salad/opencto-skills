# World-Class Sales & Revenue Generation Playbook — Full Reference

## 1. Sales Mindset

- Sell as a problem-solver, not a persuader.
- Prioritise active listening and deep discovery.
- Manage to revenue outcomes, not activity volume.
- Follow-up with value; persistence wins.

## 2. Prospecting and Lead Generation

### ICP Requirements

- Firmographics: industry, size, region, growth stage
- Technographics: core stack and integration context
- Behavioral signals: hiring, funding, leadership changes
- Buying committee map: economic buyer, technical buyer, champion, users
- Negative ICP: who not to pursue

### Channel strategy

- Outbound email: personalised multi-touch cadence
- Calling: high-speed response to trigger events
- LinkedIn: relationship-led social selling
- Referrals: highest-conversion source
- ABM: high-value account focus
- Events/webinars: thought leadership pipeline
- Inbound SEO/content: long-term compounding channel

### Qualification frameworks

- BANT: budget/authority/need/timeline
- MEDDPICC: enterprise complexity and risk reduction
- SPIN: discovery depth and pain expansion
- Challenger: insight-led commercial teaching

## 3. Pipeline Management

### Stage model

1. Prospecting
2. Initial contact
3. Discovery
4. Qualified opportunity
5. Needs analysis/demo
6. Proposal/pricing
7. Negotiation
8. Closed won/lost

### Pipeline health metrics

- Pipeline coverage ratio (target 3–4x)
- Win rate (segment by source/deal type)
- Stage conversion rates
- Average deal size
- Sales cycle length
- Pipeline velocity

### Operating practices

- Weekly pipeline review with committed next steps
- Kill stalled deals quickly
- Multi-thread enterprise opportunities
- Use mutual action plans for close planning

## 4. Negotiation

### Core rules

- Prepare BATNA and stakeholder map before negotiation
- Anchor on value and cost of inaction
- Never make unilateral concessions
- Use silence and conditional concessions strategically

### Common objection handling

- Price: benchmark against impact and alternatives
- Timing: surface hidden blockers and decision process gaps
- Competition: force explicit decision criteria comparison
- Budget: separate true budget constraints from weak priority

## 5. Closing

- Closing is outcome of disciplined discovery and deal control
- Use trial closes to detect hidden blockers
- Use summary and assumptive closes where alignment exists
- Use mutual action plan closes for complex buying committees

## 6. Account Management and Expansion

- Treat post-sale as start of growth cycle
- Use onboarding, adoption reviews, QBRs, and executive check-ins
- Start renewal planning 90+ days in advance
- Drive upsell from value realisation and usage signals
- Drive cross-sell from adjacent use-cases and peer-account patterns

## 7. Forecasting

### Methods

- Opportunity-stage weighted
- Historical conversion weighted
- Bottom-up rep commits
- AI-assisted predictive forecasting

### Cadence and controls

- Weekly forecast reviews
- Separate pipeline from forecast categories
- Track forecast accuracy by rep and segment
- Remove zombie deals aggressively

## 8. CRM Discipline

- CRM is source of truth
- Mandatory fields by stage
- Required next-step owner/date per active deal
- Required win/loss reason at close
- Ongoing duplicate/stale data remediation

## 9. Relationship Building

- Build trust through reliability, competence, integrity, and empathy
- Add value outside transactions
- Maintain executive and operational relationships in parallel
- Use social proof and references to reduce perceived risk

## 10. KPI Reference

- ARR/MRR growth
- NRR and churn
- CAC and LTV:CAC
- Quota attainment
- Lead response time
- Win rate and cycle length
- Forecast accuracy

## Practical Rule

Pipeline is oxygen.
Relationships are the moat.
Process is the advantage.
